Cultivation: Coffee plants are mainly grown in tropical regions around the world, with Brazil being the largest producer followed by Vietnam and Colombia. The plants require specific conditions, such as a suitable climate, altitude, rainfall, and soil type, to thrive. Processing: After the coffee cherries are harvested, they undergo processing to remove the outer layers and extract the coffee beans. There are two primary methods of processing: the dry method (natural process) and the wet method (washed process). Each method contributes to the coffee's flavor profile.

Brewing coffee refers to the process of extracting the flavors, aromas, and compounds from coffee grounds to create a cup of coffee. It involves combining ground coffee with water in a specific manner to achieve the desired strength and taste.
